Deer

Deer are the only group of animals in the world to have antlers. Antlers are the fastest growing living tissue in the world!

  • Scientific Name: Odocoileus virginianus
  • Average Length: 1.6 to 2.3 meters
  • Average Lifespan: 14 years
  • Habitat: Terrestrial habitats

Deer are hoofed ruminant mammals of the family Cervidae. They are native to all continents except Australia and Antarctica. There are over 60 different species of deer worldwide, and they can live in a range of habitats, from mountainous areas to warm and wet rainforests. The size and lifespan of a deer vary depending on the species and environmental factors. Some species of deer can live up to 14 years in the wild.
